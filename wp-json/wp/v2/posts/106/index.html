Mikel Arteta insists he is happy at Arsenal after being linked with a move to Paris Saint-Germain.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

The Spaniard guided the Gunners to a second-placed finish in the Premier League last season, while he won the FA Cup during his first season in charge.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

PSG, meanwhile, have been searching for a new manager following the sacking of Christophe Galtier earlier this month.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

Former Barcelona and Spain head coach Luis Enrique is closing in on a move to the French capital to replace Galtier, but reports have claimed that PSG held talks with Arteta about the possibility of a move.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

When asked about PSG\u2019s interest, Arteta told Marca: \u2018I can only say that I am happy at Arsenal.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

\u2018I feel loved, valued by our owners, Stan and Josh [Kroenke], and I have a lot to do here at this club.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

\u2018I am happy and tremendously grateful to be at Arsenal.\u2019<\/p>\n\n\n\n

Mikel Arteta insists Arsenal can win the Premier League title next season (Getty)
Asked if he would consider managing Barcelona or Real Madrid, Arteta replied: \u2018Spain is my country. I have been out for many years and at some point it could be an option to return to the league and have other experiences.\u2019<\/p>\n\n\n\n

Arteta is also adamant that Arsenal will challenge for the Premier League title next season.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

\u2018If not, I wouldn\u2019t be sitting here. That\u2019s our ambition,\u2019 said the Arsenal manager.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

\u2018So much quality, so much organization, so many resources, so many good coaches\u2026 and now Pochettino [at Chelsea] and Iraola [at Bournemouth] arrive.<\/p>\n\n\n\n
