Speaking to L\u2019\u00c9quipe, Olympique Lyonnais\u2019 Bradley Barcola (20) has described playing alongside Alexandre Lacazette (32) as \u201can honour.\u201d<\/p>\n\n\n\n

Lacazette, who came through the ranks at Lyon, returned to his formative club on a free transfer last summer. In his first season back with Les Gones, the former Arsenal striker scored an impressive 27 goals in Ligue 1; only Kylian Mbapp\u00e9 scored more.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

In part, Lacazette has Barcola to thank for reaching such a total. The forward, who is currently on France U21 duty, broke into the squad in the second half of the season, making an immediate impact. By the end of the season, Barcola had registered an impressive nine assists and scored five goals.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

Barcola has waxed lyrical about his teammate, who during his first spell with OL, was a prolific striker, whilst the former was coming through the club\u2019s academy system. \u201cIt\u2019s an honour [to play with him]. When I was young, I admired him. I was in the academy and I was 13 years old and I would never have believed that I\u2019d play alongside him. Just playing with him is mad for me. So winning with him, making him score, it\u2019s exceptional,\u201d said Barcola.<\/p>\n\n\n\n
